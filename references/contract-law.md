# Sports Contract Law — Key Frameworks

## Types of Contracts in Sports

### 1. Player Employment Contracts
- Must comply with national labor law AND governing body regulations
- FIFA: Minimum/maximum contract duration rules (RSTP Art. 18)
  - Minimum: 1 season, Maximum: 5 years (under-18: max 3 years in most associations)
- Must specify: salary, bonuses, image rights, termination conditions
- **Just cause**: Either party may terminate with just cause without financial penalty
- **Without just cause**: Terminating party owes compensation (calculated per RSTP Art. 17)
- **Sporting just cause**: Player can terminate if not playing enough (50% rule)

### 2. Transfer Agreements
- Must be registered on FIFA TMS (Transfer Matching System) for international transfers
- Key clauses to check:
  - Sell-on clause (percentage of future transfer)
  - Buyback clause
  - Loan conditions
  - Conditional transfer fees (linked to appearances, goals, etc.)
  - Payment schedule and default provisions
- Third-party ownership (TPO) of transfer rights: **Prohibited** (RSTP Art. 18ter)

### 3. Agent/Intermediary Contracts
- FIFA Football Agent Regulations (FFAR) — effective January 2023
  - Agents must be licensed by their national association
  - Commission caps: 3% for player representation, 3% for club representation (max 6% combined)
  - Dual representation prohibited unless written consent
  - Must be registered on FIFA's Football Agent Management System (FAMS)
- Always check national association implementation of FFAR

### 4. Sponsorship & Commercial Contracts
- Must comply with competition sponsor exclusivity rules
- UEFA/FIFA official partner categories restrict certain competitor brands
- Shirt sponsorship: check governing body logo/kit regulations
- Naming rights: stadium name changes require notification to governing body

### 5. Image Rights Contracts
- Separate from employment contract (reduces tax/social charges in some jurisdictions)
- Must be genuine and proportionate — tax authorities scrutinize heavily
- Governed by national law (intellectual property + employment law)

### 6. Loan Agreements
- Governed by RSTP Art. 10
- Maximum 1 loan per year between same clubs (for same player)
- Cannot loan to club in same national league (check local rules)
- Loan-to-buy options must comply with transfer regulations

## Key Compliance Checklist for Club Contracts

☐ Contract registered with national federation within required timeframe  
☐ International transfer registered on FIFA TMS (if applicable)  
☐ Agent properly licensed and registered in FAMS  
☐ No TPO provisions  
☐ Minor player: Article 19 exception obtained (if international transfer)  
☐ Image rights agreement is genuine and proportionate  
☐ Termination and compensation clauses are legally sound  
☐ Dispute resolution clause points to correct jurisdiction (CAS / national courts)  
☐ Competition regulations do not prohibit certain contract structures

## Common Red Flags in Sports Contracts

⚠️ **Side letters**: Undisclosed payments or conditions = potential fraud/ethics violation  
⚠️ **Guaranteed sell-on to third parties**: Could constitute TPO  
⚠️ **Excessive image rights split**: Tax risk + potential hidden remuneration  
⚠️ **Unregistered agents**: Club may be liable for agent's actions  
⚠️ **Automatic renewal clauses**: May exceed maximum duration limits  
⚠️ **Vague "just cause" definitions**: Will not hold up at DRC/CAS

## Governing Law & Dispute Resolution in Contracts
- Default for international disputes: Swiss law (CAS seat) or agreed national law
- Always specify: "Disputes shall be submitted exclusively to CAS" for international contracts
- For national contracts: Check if national federation DRC or national courts apply
- Employment-related disputes: National labor courts may have mandatory jurisdiction regardless of CAS clause
