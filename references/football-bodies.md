# Football Governing Bodies — Key Frameworks

## FIFA
**Website**: fifa.com
**Key regulations:**
- FIFA Statutes (last updated 2024)
- Regulations on the Status and Transfer of Players (RSTP) — governs all transfers
- FIFA Disciplinary Code — sanctions, match fixing, misconduct
- FIFA Code of Ethics
- FIFA Financial Regulations
- Regulations Governing International Matches
**Key bodies**: Disciplinary Committee, Ethics Committee, Appeal Committee, Players' Status Committee (PSC)
**Dispute resolution**: FIFA Dispute Resolution Chamber (DRC) → CAS on appeal

## UEFA
**Website**: uefa.com
**Key regulations:**
- UEFA Statutes
- UEFA Financial Sustainability Regulations (FSR) — replaced FFP in 2023
  - Squad Cost Rule: max 70% of revenues on player costs
  - Anchor Rule: no more than €5m deficit over 3 years (or up to €60m with owner investment)
- UEFA Club Competitions Regulations
- UEFA Disciplinary Regulations
- UEFA Infrastructure Regulations (stadium standards)
**Key bodies**: Club Financial Control Body (CFCB), UEFA Control, Ethics and Disciplinary Body (CEDB)
**Dispute resolution**: UEFA Appeals Body → CAS

## CAF
**Website**: cafonline.com
**Key regulations:**
- CAF Statutes
- CAF Disciplinary Code
- CAF Financial Regulations
- CAF Club Licensing Regulations
- CAF Competition Regulations (AFCON, CAF Champions League, Confederation Cup)
**Key bodies**: CAF Disciplinary Board, CAF Appeals Board

## Continental Confederations

- Spain: RFEF (rfef.es) — LaLiga (laliga.com)

## Key Transfer Rules (RSTP Summary)
- **Transfer window**: Each national association sets dates; must comply with FIFA windows
- **Training compensation**: Clubs that train players age 12-23 are owed compensation on first professional contract and transfers before age 23
- **Solidarity mechanism**: 5% of any transfer fee distributed to training clubs (proportional to years trained)
- **Third-party ownership (TPO)**: Prohibited under RSTP Article 18ter
- **Minors**: International transfers of under-18 players heavily restricted (RSTP Article 19)
- **TMS**: All international transfers must be registered on FIFA Transfer Matching System

## Club Licensing (general principles)
Clubs in UEFA competitions must hold a valid UEFA license, issued by their national federation.
Key criteria: Sporting, Infrastructure, Personnel & Administrative, Legal, Financial.
Failure = exclusion from competition.
