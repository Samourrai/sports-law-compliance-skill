# Olympic Committee & WADA

## IOC — International Olympic Committee
**Website**: olympics.com / ioc.org

### Key Frameworks
- **Olympic Charter**: Fundamental law of the Olympic movement; defines roles of IOC, NOCs, IFs
- **Host City Contract**: Obligations of host cities and organizing committees
- **Eligibility Rules**: Defined by each International Federation (IF), not IOC directly
- **Rule 40**: Restrictions on athlete/club advertising during Olympic periods
- **Refugee Athlete Program**: Eligibility for stateless or displaced athletes

### Dispute Resolution
- First instance: Each IF's internal bodies
- Appeal: CAS Ad Hoc Division during Games; regular CAS Appeals Division outside Games

## WADA — World Anti-Doping Agency
**Website**: wada-ama.org

### World Anti-Doping Code (WADC)
Current version: **WADC 2021** (effective January 1, 2021)
All signatories (IFs, NOCs, WADA) must comply.

### 10 Anti-Doping Rule Violations (ADRVs)
1. Presence of prohibited substance in sample
2. Use or attempted use of prohibited substance/method
3. Evading/refusing/failing to submit to sample collection
4. Whereabouts failures (3 strikes in 12 months)
5. Tampering with doping control
6. Possession of prohibited substance/method
7. Trafficking or attempted trafficking
8. Administration or attempted administration
9. Complicity
10. Prohibited association

### Prohibited List
Updated annually (January 1 each year) — available at wada-ama.org/en/prohibited-list
Categories: S0–S9 (substances), M1–M3 (methods), P1 (specified substances in specific sports)

### Key Principle: Strict Liability
A club/athlete cannot argue they didn't know. Presence = violation. However:
- **No significant fault or negligence** can reduce sanctions
- **Substantial assistance** (informing on others) can also reduce

### Sanctions (standard)
- First violation: 4 years (reduced to 2 years if no significant fault, or substance is specified)
- Second violation: lifetime ban possible

### Therapeutic Use Exemptions (TUEs)
Athletes needing prohibited substances for medical reasons must apply in advance through their IF or NADO (National Anti-Doping Organization).

### Club Responsibilities
- Educate athletes on prohibited substances
- Cooperate with testing authorities
- Not employ or associate with individuals under doping sanctions
- Report any known doping violations

## National Anti-Doping Organizations (NADOs)
Each country has a NADO. Examples:
- France: AFLD (afld.fr)
- UK: UK Anti-Doping (ukad.org.uk)
- USA: USADA (usada.org)
- Morocco: AMDOC
