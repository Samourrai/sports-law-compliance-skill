# CAS / TAS — Court of Arbitration for Sport

**Website**: cas-crt.org
**Full name**: Court of Arbitration for Sport / Tribunal Arbitral du Sport
**Seat**: Lausanne, Switzerland

## Role
CAS is the supreme arbitration body for international sports disputes. Most governing body regulations provide for CAS as the final appeal instance. CAS awards are enforceable under the New York Convention.

## Divisions
- **Ordinary Arbitration Division**: Commercial disputes, contract disputes between clubs, players, agents
- **Appeals Arbitration Division**: Appeals against decisions of sports federations/bodies
- **Ad Hoc Division**: Set up at Olympic Games and major events for rapid rulings (24h)

## Key Procedural Points for Club Managers
- **Appeal deadline**: Generally **21 days** from notification of the challenged decision (check applicable regulations — some bodies set 10 or 30 days)
- **Filing**: Submit Statement of Appeal + appeal brief to CAS
- **Language**: English or French (other languages by agreement)
- **Seat**: Lausanne (Swiss law governs procedural matters)
- **Cost**: Filing fee ~1,000 CHF; CAS advances costs; losing party typically bears costs

## When to go to CAS
1. You have exhausted internal remedies of the governing body (mandatory)
2. The decision is appealable under the body's statutes
3. You have a genuine legal argument (CAS is not a second chance to re-argue facts)

## Important CAS Jurisprudence Concepts
- **Proportionality**: Sanctions must be proportionate to the offense (key defense argument)
- **Strict liability**: In doping cases, intent is irrelevant — presence of substance = violation
- **De novo review**: CAS can review facts and law afresh (not just procedural errors)
- **FIFA DRC precedents**: CAS regularly reviews FIFA Dispute Resolution Chamber decisions

## How to Search CAS Jurisprudence
1. Go to cas-crt.org → Jurisprudence → Digest of CAS Awards
2. Search by keyword, sport, or governing body
3. Awards are numbered: e.g., CAS 2023/A/XXXX (A = Appeal, O = Ordinary, OG = Olympic)

## TAS (French name)
TAS = Tribunal Arbitral du Sport. Same body, French abbreviation used in French-language contexts and Francophone jurisdictions (e.g., CAF disputes).
